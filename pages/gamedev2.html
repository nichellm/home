Metro Mystery Game Document Design

Game Story

The original concept started with a story about two competitive teenage cousins who tour Washington D.C. but it evolved into the missing person mystery about one of the boys who is a avid conspiracy theorist who gets separated during a class trip. Using the popularity of the anime character from the franchise Conan the story allows the players to act the hero in solving the case. The game begins with the below script.

“During a field trip to Washington D.C. a classmate has gone missing! And it's up to Conan to find them. The classmate Finch is really into conspiracy theories and Conan thinks he may have runaway from the class trip to prove some of the theories right. Just before he disappeared from the class Finch was raving about aliens, puppets in the white house, spies, and stolen artifacts. Maybe these are clues to find him?

Game Inventory

Background Themes

A photo’s of each location was personally shot scenes using a Nikon Camera. By personalizing the test adventure in this way it serves to purposes, putting the player more into the headspace of being in those environments at eye view and it caters to the theme that Conan, the main character, is using his camera to take pictures of evidence and around the city since he too is a tourist of the area.

States

This is a space café on Mars that happens to be housed inside of a humongous Venus Flytrap. The player is a hungry space citizen and Pete, our harmless looking plant alien, is both our waiter and the owner who runs the café VERY sustainably. To reduce overharvesting of resources across the galaxy Pete has customers pick planet #’s at random to source their food ingredients from. Left over food from customers even cycles into keeping up the establishment to pay rent (feed the Venus Fly Trap holding the Cafe). If Pete can’t guess your numbered planet, he can’t be sustainable, and then there won’t be any leftovers to maintain the café. However, if Pete does guess your planet #, you get to select your ingredients from the market shelves of the planet you picked using Pete’s portal gun.

Level 1-2 Intersectionality

They both have the theme of being “slice of space life” that pairs the number convention with location and selection with retrieval. Without picking a planet in level 1 players wouldn’t have a planet market to visit in level 2 to obtain ingredients for their meal. Again, Pete is very sustainable (and possibly cheap on labor) so he’ll cook what ever you like but you also have to play a larger part in orchestrating the meal you get.
